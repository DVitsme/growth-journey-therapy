Previously we have talked about the commitment that parents have with their children’s education, but there is a very important part that we cannot leave aside. Today we want to dedicate this space to them, the children, who are today’s students. You students also have a commitment.

We often see parents who are working very hard to ensure that their children have a better future that they are prepared to face the challenges that life will throw at them. However, we also see desperate parents, whose efforts to be present in their children’s education feel like ”throwing coins into a broken purse.”

It is your turn; as a child and a student you have duties to fulfill. Creating a habit of organization at an early age will help you have a more organized life. As a student, you have certain commitments, among them:

## 1- Organizing your day.

When I tell you, organize your day, this is where it all starts. Why, you ask? Simple, when you plan what you are going to do during your day you will make better use of your time. You will have the space to study, but also to have fun. Little by little, you will create healthy habits that will help you, not only to be a better student, but also to build yourself as a person.

Design your own weekly schedule; identify what you will do each day and how long each activity will take. It will be a great advantage for you, and you will have control of your time. Here is an example:

You can distribute your time as you like, but in an efficient way, where you take into account your priorities. Remember, time is money and it is the best investment you can make in your studies.

## 2-Getting up early for school

Getting up early, I know, school age, especially adolescence, is a burst of energy, and you would like to stay up late. Your parents would also like to do the same, but they have responsibilities to attend to. Likewise, school is your responsibility, and you are the one who has to worry more about getting there early and getting your schoolwork done.

Before going to bed, leave everything you are going to use the next day ready, your clothes, shoes and backpack. Go to bed early and when you are in bed disconnect from the TV, tablet and cell phone, etc., take advantage and rest.

If you find it difficult to get up on time, set an alarm on your cell phone, at the first wake up, get up, do not postpone it and leave it for later.

Please make an effort. You will see that it will be easier to get up on time. Start your day with energy.

## 3- Doing your school work

Check at the end of the school day what work you have pending to include in your daily plan (the schedule for daily organization). When you plan your day, remember to dedicate one to two hours to study those subjects where you have more difficulty. At home, select a space where you are going to do your homework, make sure it has good lighting and avoid distractions.

Select the study techniques that are easiest for you when studying a subject, among these are: summarizing, underlining, mind mapping, concept mapping, note taking, etc. You can also record yourself before an exposition or when studying vocabulary in a different language. It will help you to have an overview of your performance.

Turn in your work on time. Be informed, and strive for quality work, try to do your best. Very important, if you do not understand something in your class, ask your teachers, they will clarify your doubts. Make use of libraries, there is a world of information there. Identify your learning style, many learn by practicing, others by listening and some are more visual. Make use of tutorials, specialized apps for study, for example: Duolingo (for languages), geogebra (for mathematics), among others.

## 4- Maintaining proper school and home behavior

Sometimes the way you behave at home, the same way you will behave at school. However, if you pay attention, your parents set rules to maintain order and peace at home. Cooperate with the housework, pick up your clothes, make your bed, keep your space tidy and clean. Cooperate with your parents, because they do their best to make sure you are well and have a home to return to after school.

When you are finally in school, it is very important that you follow the rules, they are there to keep order and to help you learn (it seems like I am preaching to you, but I am not). Respect class schedules and follow the classroom rules.

Your safety and the safety of others depends on respecting the rules. Do not give in to peer pressure; if you are encouraged to do something you know is not right, learn to say no.

At this stage of your life, which I know is not easy, and you have a thousand things, your only commitment is to study. The best thing about all this is that you study, but at the same time you share with your friends, you create bonds that will last a lifetime. For this and many other reasons, having a good behavior favors a school climate in which you can learn and enjoy yourself.

## 5- Setting your short and long-term goals

It is easier to achieve your goals when you are clear about where you want to go. To do this you must have a plan, I suggest you take into account the following: What? What you want to achieve, your goal. How? The steps to follow to reach that goal. When? How long it will take you to reach the established goal. You can follow these steps both for a goal you want to achieve in a school period, as well as for long-term projects, for example, going to college.

Sounds difficult, does not it? However, it is something that will benefit you one hundred percent. Dear student, son, this is for you; it is your turn. Evaluate the opportunities you have by studying. Stay away from situations that could affect your future. Do it for you, because what you are building you will either enjoy or regret.

If your parents support you, they are looking out for you, what better reward than to fulfill your duty. You have the time to do it, take advantage of this stage. You will learn things of great value; remember that now it is your turn. If the opposite situation occurs, you do not have support, or you feel alone in this path of studying, in the educational environment, there are people who are there to help you. Schools have programs that help you improve your academic performance, and provide emotional support when you need it. It is your turn, trust in your potential, be resilient, take care of yourself, value yourself, study, and prepare yourself.

This is a road that seems long, but every step is an advance towards your goal.

You can do it!
