This you need to know. Change is often difficult for all family members. A move, a new job, a change of school make it necessary to adapt to this new reality. These changes affect us socially, emotionally and psychologically.

Cultural deprivation, which in simple terms refers to limitations in learning due to cultural and socioeconomic shocks that affect some children. Many times teachers and families look for answers, unaware that many learning ”problems” are not due to factors other than the environment itself, and the beliefs with which each family comes from.

It is important that both families and professionals working with our children keep these characteristics in mind in order to be able to provide solutions to these possible difficulties.

If we realize that our children are underachieving in school due to cultural deprivation, here are some tips to help them:

1.  Work on the emotional part: seek help from specialists and provide the necessary support at home.
2.  Recognize their motivations: What do they like or dislike?
3.  Identify his or her learning style and plan activities based on this: How does he or she learn? What resources are best for him or her?
4.  Provide a guide or role model: this can be a friend to help him or her better understand the tasks.
5.  Although cultural deprivation is a relatively new term, you can get information from reliable sources that will help you collaborate in your child’s educational process. To learn more, write to us.
